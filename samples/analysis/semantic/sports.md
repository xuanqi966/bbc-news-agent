# Style notes: sports

## Tone & voice
The tone is authoritative and objective, yet it frequently adopts a dramatic, high-stakes narrative voice. It balances clinical reporting of facts with evocative descriptions of performance, often using a 'judgmental' lens to evaluate success or failure (e.g., describing a team as 'hapless' or 'shambolic').

## Structure & framing
Articles typically lead with a summary of the immediate result or news break, followed by a chronological or thematic breakdown. A recurring framing device is the 'historical context' or 'consequence' section, where current events are measured against past records or future implications, such as European qualification or relegation.

## Vocabulary & register
The register is a blend of formal journalism and specialized sports jargon. It utilizes vivid, aggressive verbs and adjectives to describe competition—words like 'dismantled,' 'ruthless,' 'demoralising,' and 'battering' are common. It also employs specific administrative and technical terminology when discussing the 'business' of sport, such as 'gardening leave' or 'pro-rata basis.'

## Sourcing & attribution
Sourcing is highly transparent, relying on a mix of official club/organization statements, direct quotes from press conferences, and 'BBC understands' style insider reporting. There is a heavy reliance on former players or experts to provide 'color' and emotional weight to the analysis.

## Distinctive markers
The style frequently uses alliteration (e.g., 'dogged determination,' 'second-half demolition') and metaphorical language related to warfare or natural disasters ('floodgates opened,' 'terminal to their confidence'). It also makes extensive use of bulleted sidebars or 'Read More' links to provide deep-dive context without cluttering the main narrative.

## Example phrases
- "inflicting a second successive home hammering"
- "mired in a relegation battle"
- "silence has been deafening"
- "ruthlessly dismantled Javi Gracia's hapless side"
- "legacy of excellence"
- "potential financial windfall"
