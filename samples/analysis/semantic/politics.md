# Style notes: politics

## Tone & voice
The tone is detached, analytical, and scrupulously neutral, typical of a public service broadcaster. It avoids sensationalism, even when describing 'chaos' or 'fury,' by grounding those terms in specific parliamentary actions. The voice acts as a 'constitutional referee,' focusing on the mechanics of power, the adherence to convention, and the impact of policy on the electorate.

## Structure & framing
Articles frequently use a 'claim vs. reality' framework, often employing a 'What does this mean?' or 'Is this really...?' interrogative style to deconstruct political rhetoric. They often feature chronological 'how it unfolded' timelines or bulleted lists of MPs and key points to provide a quick-reference guide to complex political events.

## Vocabulary & register
The register is formal yet accessible, blending high-level political terminology (e.g., 'parliamentary convention,' 'the whip,' 'shadow cabinet') with plain-English explanations for the layperson. It frequently uses financial and legal metaphors to describe political capital, such as 'mandate,' 'breach of commitment,' and 'giveaway.'

## Sourcing & attribution
Heavy reliance on official statements, social media posts from politicians, and 'the BBC understands' for breaking internal news. It prioritizes non-partisan third-party validation from think tanks like the Institute for Fiscal Studies (IFS) and the Resolution Foundation to provide an objective counterweight to government figures.

## Distinctive markers
A recurring feature is the 'Constituency/Majority/First elected' sidebar for individual MPs, which contextualizes their political standing. The writing also frequently references historical precedents (e.g., 'Blair-era returnee,' 'highest... since the 1940s') to frame current events within a broader temporal arc.

## Example phrases
- "the BBC understands"
- "descended into farce"
- "breaking with parliamentary convention"
- "a mandate for doing politics differently"
- "straightforward breach of their manifesto commitment"
- "ignited some speculation in Westminster"
