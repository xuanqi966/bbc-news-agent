# Style notes: technology

## Tone & voice
The tone is analytical and cautionary, often adopting a 'skeptical observer' persona. It balances objective reporting with a sense of urgency regarding digital harms. The voice is authoritative yet accessible, frequently using first-person perspective ('I flicked through', 'I learned when') to ground abstract tech concepts in personal investigation or direct experience.

## Structure & framing
Articles often frame technology through the lens of 'crisis' or 'unprecedented change.' A common structural technique is the 'demonstration-to-implication' flow, where a specific incident (a lawsuit, a viral post, or a voice clone) serves as a springboard for broader discussions on industry ethics, regulatory failure, or geopolitical shifts. Bulleted lists and short, punchy paragraphs are used to break down complex technical consequences.

## Vocabulary & register
The register is a blend of specialized tech terminology and evocative, almost cinematic metaphors. It uses industry-specific terms like 'geo-engineering,' 'USB-C,' and 'SSL certificates' alongside dramatic descriptors like 'meteoric rise,' 'plunged into crisis,' and 'pinning their political colours to the mast.' This creates a 'high-stakes' atmosphere for technical news.

## Sourcing & attribution
Sourcing is diverse, typically triangulating between corporate statements, academic experts (e.g., University of Surrey), and specialized think tanks (e.g., Institute of Strategic Dialogue). There is a heavy reliance on 'BBC Verify' or independent verification attempts, often explicitly noting when a claim cannot be confirmed or when a company (like X or Amazon) has failed to respond to requests for comment.

## Distinctive markers
A recurring marker is the 'future-casting' question used as a transition or conclusion ('So will these latest allegations hurt...?', 'Is internet on verge of break-up?'). The articles also frequently reference the BBC’s own past reporting to establish a timeline of expertise, and they often include 'call-to-action' links to related BBC podcasts or radio segments.

## Example phrases
- "plunged the show into crisis"
- "pinning their political colours to the mast"
- "social media universe that rewards engagement over truth"
- "dangerous slippery slope towards what is known as the Splinternet"
- "fears about AI-powered audio-fakery up to fever pitch"
