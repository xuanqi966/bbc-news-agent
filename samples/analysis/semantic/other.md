# Style notes: other

## Tone & voice
The tone is consistently neutral, informative, and detached, even when reporting on sensational or tragic events. It maintains a 'view from nowhere' by balancing conflicting viewpoints without taking a side. In human-interest stories, the voice becomes slightly more conversational and lighthearted, often incorporating gentle puns or colloquialisms to engage the reader.

## Structure & framing
Articles typically follow an 'inverted pyramid' or a 'round-up' format. News summaries (like the 'Newspaper headlines' series) use a thematic structure, grouping different publications' takes on a single issue before moving to the next topic. Individual stories often lead with a striking statistic or a direct consequence of the event before providing context.

## Vocabulary & register
The register is accessible but formal (Standard British English). It avoids jargon, and when technical terms like 'colostrum' or 'cryotherapy' are used, they are immediately defined. The vocabulary for political reporting is descriptive and focuses on action and reaction (e.g., 'overhaul', 'thwart', 'tackle').

## Sourcing & attribution
Attribution is rigorous and frequent. Every claim from a third party is clearly tagged with phrases like 'it reports,' 'the paper says,' or 'according to.' Direct quotes are used to provide 'color' or personal perspective, while the BBC's own voice remains restricted to factual connective tissue.

## Distinctive markers
A hallmark is the use of 'external' links and 'click here' calls to action for digital navigation. The 'Newspaper headlines' format specifically uses a 'paws for thought' style of transition to move from heavy political news to lighthearted 'and finally' style segments at the end of the piece.

## Example phrases
- "dredged from the sewers of political muckraking"
- "gives its readers paws for thought"
- "once in a career, probably lifetime, experience"
- "doesn’t know how famous she is"
- "pave the way for a new type of 'acoustic farming'"
- "the unique human stories behind current events"
