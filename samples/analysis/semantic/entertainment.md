# Style notes: entertainment

## Tone & voice
The tone is authoritative yet accessible, balancing journalistic objectivity with the enthusiasm of a fan. It maintains a neutral, observational stance even when describing high-drama events (like the 'Willy Wonka' fiasco) or high-stakes awards, using a voice that is informative and culturally literate without becoming overly colloquial.

## Structure & framing
Articles typically lead with a record-breaking achievement or a significant event (the 'hook'), followed by a breakdown of statistics or chronological context. Lists and 'best of' roundups are common. Framing often centers on historical significance, such as comparing current achievements against past records or milestones (e.g., 100 years of fashion or 60 years since The Beatles' first win).

## Vocabulary & register
The register is standard British English with a mix of industry-specific terminology (e.g., 'citations,' 'record of the year,' 'diss track') and evocative adjectives. It uses descriptive labels to categorize artists and events, such as 'flirtatious summer anthem' or 'volatile pop masterclass,' to provide quick critical shorthand for the reader.

## Sourcing & attribution
Attribution is precise, citing specific platforms (BBC Radio's Good Morning Scotland), official bodies (The Recording Academy, Glasgow City Council), and 'law enforcement sources.' Direct quotes are used strategically to provide emotional weight or to highlight conflict, often pulling from social media posts or live acceptance speeches.

## Distinctive markers
The style frequently uses 'superlative' framing (most-nominated, oldest-ever, best-selling). There is a recurring focus on the intersection of technology and entertainment, specifically mentioning AI-generated content. Short, punchy paragraphs—often only one or two sentences—are the standard for readability.

## Example phrases
- "15 pages of AI generated gibberish"
- "volatile pop masterclass"
- "dazzling and flamboyant debut"
- "scold Grammy voters"
- "sparsely-decorated warehouse"
- "meteoric rise to fame"
