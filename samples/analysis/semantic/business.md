# Style notes: business

## Tone & voice
The tone is authoritative yet empathetic, balancing macroeconomic data with the 'human cost' of business shifts. It avoids sensationalism, opting for a measured, explanatory voice that frames corporate developments through their direct impact on individual livelihoods and consumer wallets.

## Structure & framing
Articles typically follow an 'inverted pyramid' with a hard-news lead, followed by a pivot to personal testimony or expert analysis. A recurring framing device is the 'ripple effect,' where a single corporate or policy event (like a factory closure or interest rate hike) is traced through its consequences for local economies, supply chains, or household budgets.

## Vocabulary & register
The register is accessible professional. It utilizes industry-specific terminology—such as 'single-origin,' 'fixed-rate deal,' 'supply chain,' and 'installed base'—but immediately contextualizes these terms for a general audience. Metaphors are frequently used to simplify complex economic trends, particularly regarding 'crunches' or 'shocks.'

## Sourcing & attribution
Sourcing is multi-layered, consistently pairing official corporate or government statements with independent 'think tank' data (e.g., Resolution Foundation) and academic commentary. Crucially, the BBC prioritizes 'Have Your Say' style grassroots sourcing, quoting named or anonymous individuals directly affected by the news.

## Distinctive markers
The use of bulleted 'key facts' or 'what you need to know' sidebars is common. Articles often conclude with a call to action for readers to share their own experiences via WhatsApp or email, and frequently include a 'More on this story' section with links to related explainers.

## Example phrases
- "leaving the UK in a 'mortgage crunch'"
- "putting the brakes on its hardwired association"
- "shoppers squeezed by the rising cost of living"
- "a complete slap in face for our members"
- "navigating a 'challenging environment'"
- "the cocoa value chain remains unequally divided"
