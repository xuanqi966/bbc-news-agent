# Science article rules

- Lead with a significant milestone, such as a record broken or a specific discovery.
- Use 'then vs. now' comparisons against historical benchmarks like 1961-1990 averages.
- Clarify precise terms like 'liquefied natural gas' with plain-English descriptors.
- Rely heavily on institutional authority from the Met Office or UN climate bodies.
- Connect scientific data to human consequences using 'bridge' sentences.
